My first scientific paper, *Structural and topological description of the liquid-liquid transition in water*,  was recently published
(and chosen as featured) on **The Journal of Chemical Physics**:
<a href="https://doi.org/10.1063/5.0049299">https://doi.org/10.1063/5.0049299</a>

The preprint has been made freely available on <a href="https://arxiv.org/abs/2104.10144">arXiv</a>
and an associated *Scilight* publication is available <a href="https://doi.org/10.1063/10.0005052">here</a>.

Our simulations show that it is possible to obtain long-lived metastable configurations of water at coexistence between a low-density liquid and a high-density liquid (LDL and HDL),
supporting the liquid-liquid critical point scenario.
We propose a novel methodology to identify the structural transformations associated to the phase transition, based on a separation of the radial distribution function which makes use
of topological information from the underlying HB network.
The fact that we could compare two equilibrated liquid configurations at the exact same thermodynamic conditions but with different densities, allows for a clear visualization
of the characteristic features that distinguish the two liquid phases.
